Citrus

What is a Citrus?

A citrus fruit is a type of fruit that comes from trees in the genus Citrus. These fruits are typically juicy, tangy to sour in flavor, and have a high amount of vitamin C. Common citrus fruits include: Oranges Lemons Limes Grapefruits Mandarins They usually have a thick, aromatic rind (peel) and segmented flesh inside. Citrus fruits are widely used in cooking, drinks, and desserts, and they’re valued for both their flavor and nutritional benefits. Botanically, citrus fruits are a special kind of berry called a hesperidium, which is characterized by its leathery rind and juicy interior.

5 Types of Citrus

Oranges

Oranges are round, medium-sized citrus fruits with a bright orange peel. They have a juicy, sweet-to-tangy flavor and are one of the most commonly eaten fresh fruits. They’re often eaten raw, juiced, or used in desserts and savory dishes.

Scientific name:
Citrus × sinensis
Taste:
Sweet to mildly tart

Lemons

Lemons are oval-shaped with a bright yellow rind. They have a sharp, very sour taste due to high citric acid content. Because they are too sour to eat alone, they’re mainly used to flavor food, drinks, and desserts.

Scientific name:
Citrus × limon
Taste:
Strongly sour

Limes

Limes are small, round to oval green fruits. They are highly acidic, sour, and slightly bitter, with a strong citrus aroma. They are commonly used in cooking, cocktails, marinades, and sauces to add sharpness.

Scientific name:
Citrus × aurantiifolia
Taste:
Sour and acidic

Grapefruits

Grapefruits are large, round citrus fruits with thick yellow to pinkish skin. Their taste is a mix of sweet, sour, and bitter, depending on the variety. They are often eaten fresh or juiced, especially as a breakfast fruit.

Scientific name:
Citrus × paradisi
Taste:
Tart to mildly sweet

Mandarins

Mandarins are small, easy-to-peel citrus fruits with a thin orange skin. They are sweet, mild, and very fragrant with low acidity. They are commonly eaten fresh and are especially popular as snacks because of their convenience.

Scientific name:
Citrus reticulata
Taste:
Sweet and mild

Nutritional Benefits

Citrus fruits (like oranges, lemons, limes, grapefruits, and mandarins) are known for being nutrient-dense and low in calories. Their main nutritional benefits include:

Rich in antioxidants
They contain compounds like flavonoids and carotenoids that: Reduce inflammation Protect against oxidative stress May support heart health.
Hydration support
Citrus fruits have high water content, helping: Maintain hydration Support body temperature regulation and energy levels.
Heart health benefits
Potassium and flavonoids in citrus fruits may: Help regulate blood pressure Support healthy blood circulation.

Interesting Citrus Facts

  • Lemons were once rare luxury items
  • Limes helped prevent scurvy
  • Mandarins are one of the oldest citrus fruits
