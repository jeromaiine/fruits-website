Pomes

What is a Pome?

A pome is a type of fleshy fruit produced by flowering plants in the rose family (Rosaceae), most commonly seen in apples and pears. What makes a pome distinctive is its structure: the edible part you eat is not just the fruit’s ovary, but also enlarged surrounding floral tissues (called the receptacle or hypanthium). Inside the core of a pome are several small seed chambers, usually arranged in a star-like pattern, which is the true fruit part botanically. The outer flesh is typically soft and edible, while the center “core” is tougher and contains the seeds. Common examples include the apple and pear, both of which are widely cultivated and eaten around the world.

5 Types of Pomes

Apple

The apple is a widely cultivated temperate fruit produced by trees in the rose family. It comes in a wide range of sizes, colors, and textures, varying from crisp and crunchy to soft and tender.

Scientific name:
Malus domestica
Taste:
Very sweet to sharply tart depending on variety

Pear

The pear is ateardrop-shaped fruit that ripens from the inside out. Often harvested firm and allowed to soften after picking.

Scientific name:
Pyrus communis
Taste:
Mildly sweet, floral, and juicy with a soft, buttery texture when ripe

Quince

The quince is a golden-yellow, highly aromatic fruit that resembles a lumpy pear. It is rarely eaten raw due to hardness and astringency

Scientific name:
Cydonia oblonga
Taste:
Very tart, astringent, and dry

Medlar

The medlar is a small, brownish fruit traditionally eaten after “bletting” (softening through overripening). Once common in medieval Europe.

Scientific name:
Mespilus germanica
Taste:
After bletting, it tastes like spiced apple sauce, with notes of caramel, cinnamon, and fermented fruit complexity.

Loquat

The loquat is a small, oval, orange-yellow fruit growing in clusters on evergreen trees native to East Asia

Scientific name:
Eriobotrya japonica
Taste:
Sweet to mildly tart

Nutritional Benefits

Pome fruits—such as apples, pears, and quinces—are highly nutritious and offer a range of health benefits. They are rich in dietary fiber, especially soluble fiber like pectin, which supports healthy digestion, helps regulate blood sugar levels, and can contribute to lower cholesterol. These fruits are also a good source of vitamin C, which supports immune function and skin health, along with various antioxidants such as flavonoids and polyphenols that help reduce inflammation and protect cells from oxidative stress.

Rich in antioxidants
Includes flavonoids and vitamin C that help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation.
Heart health support
The fiber and polyphenols may help reduce LDL (“bad”) cholesterol and support healthy blood pressure.
High in fiber (especially pectin)
Helps digestion, supports gut health, and can improve cholesterol levels.

Interesting Pome Facts

  • Apples are extremely diverse, with over 7,500 varieties grown worldwide, ranging from sweet eating apples to tart cider apples.
  • The “core” of pome fruits is technically the true fruit, while the fleshy part we eat is accessory tissue.
  • Quinces are very hard and astringent when raw but become fragrant, soft, and often pink when cooked due to chemical changes.
